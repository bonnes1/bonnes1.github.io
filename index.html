Here is a visual mapping of various variables from “World Values survey” (WVS). You can either search for countries to see how their values changes over the years, click to filter different Culture Zones or brush the different variables y-axis for a desired span of the variable. It is also possible to move around the axises by moving the variable titles or even remove if you wish to do that by moving it to the end of either side.

    Values over time and comparison

    By searching for different countries you can notice how different values have changed over time. By searching for e.g. Sweden you can notice the values of emphasis on universal freedoms in the domain of reproductive choices (EV_choice) has drastically increased. By filtering out all but "Reformed West" (somewhat central Europe) we can see a trending pattern of values that are similar to each of these countries. Even more interesting, when comparing "Reformed West" with "Orthodox East" we can notice very different values in the matter of emphasis in the domain of universal freedoms in both reproductive choices but also people's voice.

    Overall difference in variables

    By brushing a small timespan of about 5 years and comparing from the 1980s and the 2010s you can compare all the different variables. One noticeable structure is how the countries from later years are more spread in their values compared to earlier years. E.g. how the people consider their own health is very widespread in the 2010s compared to earlier years where it seems like people from various countries would consider themselves as more healthy. The reason for this could simply be because of less data points in the 1980s compared to 2010s but if you exclude that, one reason of the pattern could be because of the environmental challenges that people are more aware of today compared to the 1980s.
